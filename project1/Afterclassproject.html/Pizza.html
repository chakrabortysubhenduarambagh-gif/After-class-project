    Use "00" flour (highly refined Italian flour) or high-protein bread flour. ComponentIngredientsThe Dough500g Flour, 325ml Warm Water, 10g Salt, 7g Instant YeastThe Sauce1 can San Marzano tomatoes, pinch of salt, splash of olive oilThe ToppingsFresh mozzarella (drained), fresh basil, extra virgin olive oil2. Prepare the DoughMix: Combine flour, yeast, and salt in a bowl. Slowly add warm water and stir until a shaggy dough forms.Knead: Move to a floured surface. Knead for 8–10 minutes until the dough is smooth, elastic, and bounces back when poked.Proof: Lightly oil a bowl, place the dough inside, and cover with a damp cloth. Let it rise in a warm spot for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until doubled in size.Divide: Punch the air out and divide into 3 or 4 equal balls. Let them rest for another 30 minutes.3. The "Secret" SauceDon't cook your sauce beforehand!Crush the canned tomatoes by hand or with a pulse blender.Add a pinch of salt and a drizzle of olive oil. That’s it. The sauce will cook perfectly in the oven, staying bright and fresh.4. Shaping and ToppingStretch, Don't Roll: Hand-stretch the dough from the center outward to keep the air in the crust (the "cornicione"). Avoid using a rolling pin, which flattens the air bubbles.The Layering: Spread a thin layer of sauce, add torn pieces of mozzarella, and a few basil leaves.Pro Tip: If using fresh mozzarella in water, slice it and let it drain on paper towels for 15 minutes first to avoid a "soupy" pizza.5. The BakeSince home ovens don't hit $450^{\circ}C$ ($850^{\circ}F$) like wood-fired ovens, you have to maximize what you have:Preheat: Set your oven to its maximum temperature (usually $260^{\circ}C$/$500^{\circ}F$) for at least 45 minutes.The Surface: Use a pizza stone or an inverted baking sheet.The Timing: Slide the pizza onto the hot surface. Bake for 8–12 minutes until the crust is charred in spots and the cheese is bubbling.Finishing TouchesAs soon as it comes out, hit it with a fresh drizzle of extra virgin olive oil and a crack of black pepper. Let it sit for 2 minutes before slicing so the cheese sets.What kind of toppings are you usually drawn to—traditional savory styles or something with a bit of a spicy or sweet kick?
